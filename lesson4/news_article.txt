FLOODS KILL 42 ACROSS KENYA, NAIROBI HARDEST HIT
Date: March 9, 2026
Source: Compiled from multiple Kenyan news outlets

At least 42 people have been killed following days of devastating floods across Kenya, with the capital Nairobi bearing the brunt of the disaster. Heavy rains triggered flash floods across several regions of the country, sweeping through low-lying neighbourhoods and informal settlements, submerging homes and carrying away vehicles as rivers burst their banks.

Kenya's Public Service and Special Programmes Minister Geoffrey Ruku confirmed the death toll to reporters, noting that 26 of the 42 deaths occurred in Nairobi alone. The floods caused extensive damage to infrastructure and livelihoods across multiple counties, with the Kenyan Red Cross reporting that hundreds of households in neighbouring counties had also been affected, and vast swaths of farmland destroyed.

President William Ruto responded by deploying a team of emergency responders, including soldiers, to coordinate rescue efforts. He ordered the immediate release and distribution of relief food from national strategic reserves to affected families. The government also committed to covering hospital bills for those injured or affected by the flooding and currently receiving treatment in public health facilities.

Scientists and climate experts have pointed out that global warming is worsening floods and droughts across East Africa by concentrating rainfall into shorter, more intense bursts. The Nairobi floods have once again highlighted what President Ruto described as "the urgent need for lasting solutions to the perennial challenge of flooding in our urban areas."

RUSSIA AGREES TO STOP RECRUITING KENYANS FOR UKRAINE WAR
Date: March 16, 2026
Source: Al Jazeera / AFP

Kenya secured a significant diplomatic agreement in Moscow on Monday when Russia agreed to halt the recruitment of Kenyan citizens to fight in the Ukraine conflict. Kenya's Foreign Minister Musalia Mudavadi made the announcement following talks with Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov at the Russian foreign ministry.

"We have now agreed that Kenyans shall not be enlisted through the Russian Ministry of Defence," Mudavadi told reporters. The agreement comes after a Kenyan intelligence report presented to lawmakers in February revealed that more than 1,000 Kenyans had been recruited to fight on Russia's side in the war in Ukraine — five times more than authorities had previously estimated. Ukraine's estimates put the total number of African fighters in Russian ranks at over 1,780 citizens from 36 African countries.

Kenyan politicians have described what they allege is a network of rogue state officials who colluded with human trafficking syndicates to recruit Kenyans to fight for Russia, a practice Nairobi has publicly sought to end. Lavrov, for his part, stated that Russia was not forcing anyone to enlist and that Russian citizens had voluntarily signed contracts. He added that the Russian Defence Ministry was looking into cases that had caused concern among Kenya's government.

Mudavadi emphasised that the Kenya-Russia relationship is broader than the Ukraine conflict. "We do not want for any reason our partnership with Russia to be defined from the lens of the special operation in Ukraine agenda only," he said.

PRESIDENT RUTO URGES DIPLOMATS TO MARKET KENYA ABROAD
Date: March 28, 2026
Source: The Star Kenya

President William Ruto opened the 19th Ambassadors' Conference in Nairobi on Saturday, calling on Kenya's diplomats and envoys to step up efforts to promote the country's economic and strategic interests abroad. Speaking to the gathering, Ruto cited improved economic stability and growing investor confidence as reasons for optimism, even as he acknowledged a shifting and uncertain global environment.

"We meet at a moment of profound change in the global order, an era marked by deep and often unpredictable disruptions," the President said. He nevertheless maintained that Kenya is well-positioned to navigate the turbulence, pointing to the country's gross domestic product of approximately $136 billion and annual economic growth of around five per cent.

Ruto told ambassadors that Kenya had avoided a projected debt default that had affected several other African countries, crediting this to deliberate fiscal reforms including expenditure controls and the removal of what he described as unsustainable subsidies. "By any credible measure — whether economic stability, fiscal discipline, or institutional resilience — the direction is unmistakable. Kenya is not only steady, but it is also on the rise," he said.

The President also reiterated Kenya's commitment to regional and global peace efforts, including ongoing engagements in Haiti and parts of Africa, while emphasising the importance of strengthening regional trade integration across the continent.

KENYA POLITICAL TENSIONS RISE AHEAD OF 2027 POLLS
Date: March 25, 2026
Source: The Online Kenyan

Kenya's political landscape is showing increasing signs of tension ahead of the 2027 general elections. Ford Kenya, led by National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetangula, has firmly rejected demands from President William Ruto's United Democratic Alliance to merge into the ruling party. The party described such calls as a betrayal of their partnership within the Kenya Kwanza coalition, emphasising Ford Kenya's historical significance and its commitment to remaining an independent political entity.

The development follows the entry of the ODM party into the ruling coalition, which created new demands around pre-election pact conditions and shifted the balance of power within the coalition. At the same time, a grim discovery in Kericho County has drawn national attention to questions around child welfare and institutional accountability. Authorities from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations exhumed 33 bodies from a mass grave at Makaburini Cemetery, with 25 of the victims confirmed to be infants.

In matters of public health, Kenya's Kericho mass grave case has prompted renewed calls for stronger oversight of health facilities and burial practices across the country. Governors have separately demanded that Universal Health Coverage funds be treated as an equitable share of revenue rather than a conditional grant for the 2026/2027 financial year, as they continue to push for a larger share of the national budget to meet increased county-level responsibilities.

TEACHERS STRIKE AVERTED AS GOVERNMENT INTERVENES ON SHA SCHEME
Date: March 28, 2026
Source: Kenyans.co.ke

The Kenyan government stepped in at the final hour to avert a teachers' strike, intervening just as a seven-day strike notice issued by teachers over the Social Health Authority scheme was about to expire. The SHA scheme, which replaced the National Hospital Insurance Fund, has been a point of contention for public sector workers across the country, with teachers joining other groups in raising concerns about implementation and deductions.

The government's intervention came amid broader public debate about the SHA scheme's rollout. Earlier in March, the government disbursed over Ksh 15 billion to hospitals to ensure smooth operations across health centres nationwide, signalling an effort to shore up the new health financing system. However, critics, including some members of Parliament, have continued to question whether the funds are being allocated and utilised effectively at the facility level.

The SHA situation is part of a wider set of governance and public service challenges facing the Ruto administration as it enters the second half of its term, with the 2027 election horizon beginning to shape political calculations across the board.
